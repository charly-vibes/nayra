# The Name "Nayra"

**Nayra** (ᐅ nayra) is an Aymara word that means both "eye/sight" and "past" — as well as "in front of." In the Aymara language, spoken by about 2 million indigenous people in the Andean highlands of Bolivia, Peru, and Chile, time flows in the opposite direction from most Western languages: **the past is in front of you (because you can see it), and the future is behind you (because it is unknown).**

For example:

- **"Nayra mara"** means "last year" (literally: the year in front)
- **"Qhipa mara"** means "next year" (literally: the year behind)

This is not just a linguistic metaphor — it extends to gesture. Aymara speakers point forward when discussing past events and wave over their shoulders when referring to the future. The farther forward they reach, the more distant the past event.

As cognitive scientist Rafael Núñez and linguist Eve Sweetser documented in their research published in *Cognitive Science*, the Aymara equate the forward direction with what is known — what has been seen by the eyes, what has already happened. Behind, where they cannot see, lies the unknown future.

## Why This Name?

Nayra is a timeline visualization tool. Its purpose is to make the past visible — to lay out history in front of you so you can see it, explore it, and understand it. The Aymara concept of *nayra* captures this perfectly:

- **Facing the past**: Nayra renders historical events as a visual landscape you look into
- **Seeing what is known**: The timeline makes the known (past events, data) visible and navigable
- **Deep time**: Like the Aymara sense of time that stretches across generations, Nayra handles timescales from microseconds to billions of years

The name is a tribute to the Aymara people and their profound insight that to understand where we are, we must face where we have been.
